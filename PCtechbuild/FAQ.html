PC Building FAQ

What components do I need to build a PC?

To build a PC, you will need a CPU, motherboard, RAM, storage, power supply, and a case. You may also need a graphics card, depending on your needs.

How do I choose the right components for my PC?

When choosing components for your PC, consider your budget, the intended use of the PC, and compatibility between components. Research each component and read reviews to make an informed decision.

How difficult is it to build a PC?

Building a PC is straightforward and requires very few tools. By following our build guide, you will be able to build your PC in an afternoon. And once you’ve built your PC you will be able to customise and upgrade it yourself - saving you time and money in future.

Can I switch out the GPU with another model?

Yes, GPUs are (almost) universally compatible. There’s no need to match the motherboard and GPU manufacturer.

Can I switch the monitor for another?

Yes. This is one of the simplest swaps you can make to your PC, and any modern monitor will be compatible with the GPUs in these builds.

Can I use a gaming build to stream from?

All of the gaming builds are capable of streaming your gameplay on platforms such as Twitch without additional hardware.

Should I just buy a prebuilt PC instead?

this is not always the case with a PC sold pre-assembled. Where prebuilt computers will often skimp on components like cases and power supplies, every part of our builds has been chosen to offer the best performance for your budget so we also recommend choosing a prebuilt.

Why do we list performance of builds in the prebuilt area?

to give you the user a better understanding and to make sure that you have choosing the right PC for your need.

How much does it cost to build a gaming PC

The cost can vary depending on the components you choose, but a decent gaming PC can be built for around 4000-5000 SAR.

Do I have to build my PC myself??

No, you can buy pre-built PCs, but building your own can be a fun and rewarding experience.

What are some common mistakes to avoid when building a PC?

Common mistakes include not selecting compatible components, not properly installing components, and not properly managing cables no need to worry about compatibilty here.

Is building a PC difficult?

Building a PC can be complex, but it is not necessarily difficult. With proper research and preparation, anyone can build a PC.

What tools do I need to build a PC?

You will need a screwdriver, thermal paste, and possibly cable ties and pliers.

Is building a PC worth it?

Building a PC can be worth it if you enjoy the process and want to customize your computer to your specific needs. However, pre-built PCs can be a more convenient option for some people.

Why do gamers need to buy dual-channel RAM?

Dual-channel RAM can improve gaming performance by allowing the CPU to access data from two memory modules simultaneously.

How much video memory is enough for gaming?

The amount of video memory needed for gaming depends on the resolution and quality settings being used. 4GB is generally sufficient for 1080p gaming, while 8GB or more may be needed for higher resolutions.

How much RAM do you recommend?

The amount of RAM needed depends on the intended use of the computer. For gaming and general use, 8GB is usually sufficient, while more demanding applications may require 16GB or more.

How often should you upgrade your system?

It depends on your needs and budget. Upgrading every 3-5 years is common, but some people may upgrade more frequently or less frequently.

How do I install an operating system on my new PC?

You can install an operating system using a USB drive or DVD, and you will need to enter the BIOS to select the boot device.

How do I manage cables when building a PC?

You can use cable ties and routing holes in the case to manage cables and improve airflow.

What is overclocking, and should I do it?

Overclocking involves increasing the clock speed of a component to improve performance, but it can also increase heat and reduce component lifespan. Whether or not to overclock depends on your needs and preferences.

How do I troubleshoot problems when building a PC?

You can use online resources such as forums and guides to troubleshoot problems, and you can also contact technical support for the components you are using.
